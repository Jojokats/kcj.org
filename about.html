about us

our mission

We are a Canadian not for profit organization dedicated to empowering children, teachers and parents with the skills we all need to thrive in a technology driven society. By bringing digital learning experiences into the classroom and in communities, we teach Canadians how to be better thinkers and better creators in a digital world.

our history

  • Fall 2017
    Going into our 5th school year, we're excited to work with teachers and children in schools in BC, Alberta,Ontario and Quebec. And in every province and territory with our Code Clubs!
    FALL 2017
  • Spring 2017
    Kids Code Jeunesse receives federal NSERC funding for 3 years to expand operations across Canada. We are also the only Canadian organization to win a Google rise in 2017 grantto support national growth of Code, Create, Play Workshops.
    SPRING 2017
  • Fall 2016
    In partnership with Lighthouse Labs Kids Code Jeunesse wins a contract to work with the Ministry of Education in British Columbia to help integrate into the new curriculum.
    .
    FALL 2016
  • Spring 2016
    Teacher training workshops enter pilot mode in Montreal and Quebec City, and soon after, Vancouver and Toronto. We also role out our first Code, Create, Play workshops, which introduce gaming, story-telling, art & hardware with code.
    SPRING 2016
  • Fall 2015
    We partnerwith orgaizations like Hour of Code and CBC Kids to reach canadians from coast to coast, and branch out outside of Quebec with to support teachers andkids in British Columbia.
    FALL 2015
  • Spring 2015
    We bring Code Club to Canada. With clubs around the world and a 48% girl attendance rate. It's a perfect fit for our mission to increase opportunities for girls. With materials available in 20 languages, it's ideal for all canadian kids.
    SPRING 2015
  • Fall 2014
    Our first sponsor! Vigilant Global covers for 4 years in a row office rent at Notman House and support our national growth. We begin our first year mentoring teams of girls at Technovation, a global technology entrepreneurship competition.
    FALL 2014
  • Spring 2014
    Our first grant from CIRA helps us develop classroom materials for Canadian Teachers. We host our first Committee meeting, made up of primary school teachers, university professors, computer scientists and programmers.
    SPRING 2014
  • Fall 2013
    We knock on Montreal primary school doors - they open! - to start working with teachers to bring code into classrooms. We talk with corporate leaders, university professors and send LOTS of letters to provincial and federal leaders.
    FaLL 2013
  • Spring 2013
    Hello Canada! Countries around the world educate young children to code for future excellence, but in 2013 there was no national movement in Canada. Why early education? So we can engage girls before gender stereotypes set in and learning to code is like learning a new language, it's easier to learn when we start young!
    SPRING 2013
